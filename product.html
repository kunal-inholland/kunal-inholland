VRWorkout

With this new virtual reality music workout game, you will get an intense full-body workout that is on par with other calisthenics exercises. This VR game is much more engaging than others like Beat Saber and Box VR, since it involves more muscle groups by mixing up standing, squatting, pushups, side planks, crunches, jumping and burpees.

What problem does it solve?

This game makes working out a fun activity. Working out doesn't have to be a chore. Turn up the music and get moving with a fitness game that uses motion tracking. See your progress as you go, and work on your technique to improve your score. This can be a great way to stay motivated and enjoy your workout, without getting bored. The only downside is that you need a VR headset and enough space to move around without hitting anything.

How to use this?

In order to use VRWorkout, you will need a virtual reality headset that has at least two motion trackers for your hands. The Oculus Quest is the recommended headset for this game, as it can be used wirelessly. To get started, simply download the free game from Steam or the Quest Store. At first, working out in VR may cause nausea, but this will dissipate with time and eventually become enjoyable.

VR workout on the internet

Virtual reality has become a popular way to workout, with many people sharing their experiences on YouTube. One of those people is Ryan Trahan, who completed a 30 day challenge where he played different workout games in VR every day. In the span of those 30 days, he lost 3.5 kilos and got his body into much better shape. This proves the effectiveness of VR Workout. VR Workout paired with a good diet is definitely the most fun way to bring your body back in shape.

The creators of VRWorkout

With VRWorkout, you can get an immersive workout experience that is also free and open source. The app employs no sketchy data collection, so you can be sure that your privacy is respected. You can also view the code to see how it works. With great support from the community of developers, VRWorkout is always improving.
